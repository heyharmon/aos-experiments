**From:** Billing <ar@vendor-invoices.example>
**Subject:** Outstanding invoice: please remit
**Received:** 2026-06-15
**Amount requested:** $9,800 USD wire

## Why this is flagged

This email has multiple hallmarks of a business email compromise (BEC) scam:

1. Unknown sender domain. "vendor-invoices.example" does not match any known vendor.
2. No invoice number, no service description, no PO reference.
3. Requests wire to a "new" bank account effective "this month" - the #1 BEC red flag.
4. Urgency language ("remit immediately", "avoid service interruption") designed to bypass verification.

## Recommended action

Do NOT wire funds. If you believe a real vendor may have a legitimate outstanding balance, call them on a contact number already on file (not from this email) to verify before any payment.
